A Manifesto for Democratic Reclamation

Preamble. Local government exists to serve the community, protect public assets, and provide a safety net for the vulnerable. When the mechanisms of local democracy are captured by national party machinery, corporate developers, and private finance, the public interest is lost. We reject the normalisation of institutional capture, the "trickle-down" exploitation of our public land, and the silencing of local dissent. To reclaim our local democracy, we commit to the following core principles.
I

Independence Over Party Loyalty

Local issues require local solutions, not national party dogma. We reject the use of the "Party Whip" in local council chambers, which stifles independent thought and forces representatives to vote against the interests of their own constituents. We support the election of independent, unwhipped representatives who answer only to the community, and advocate for a shift to proportional representation to permanently end the one-party state.

II

Community Wealth Over Corporate Extraction

Public land and public money must serve the public good. We reject economic models that hand our town centres to private equity while cutting front-line services. We advocate for Community Wealth Building: demanding that local institutions use their procurement budgets to support local businesses, worker co-operatives, and living-wage employers — ensuring wealth circulates within our community rather than leaking to corporate shareholders.

III

Distributed Power Over Executive Control

The concentration of power in a single Mayor or a tiny Cabinet breeds complacency and allows corporate lobbying to thrive in the shadows. We demand the dismantling of the "Strong Leader" model in favour of a modern Committee System where decision-making is shared, transparent, and requires cross-party consensus. For decisions that permanently alter our borough, we demand Citizens' Assemblies to put power directly into the hands of informed residents.

IV

Relentless Scrutiny Over Blind Trust

Transparency is not a request; it is a requirement. When auditors hold financial interests in the bodies they audit, the system has failed. We commit to open-source scrutiny: utilising Freedom of Information requests, scrutinising public registers, and exercising our legal right to inspect and object to local council accounts. The four FOI targets in this dossier are a starting point — not an endpoint. We will not accept bureaucratic jargon as an excuse for financial conflicts of interest.

V

Collective Leverage Over Individual Complaints

When the council acts as an absentee landlord or ignores the needs of the vulnerable, traditional complaint forms are insufficient. We believe in the power of asymmetric direct action. We stand with Tenant Unions, community organisers, and crowdfunded legal challenges. When the internal democratic machinery is locked down, we will use our collective external leverage to force the council to the negotiating table.

The Pledge

We will no longer accept the managed decline of our public services masked by the shiny veneer of corporate real estate.

We commit to voting, organising, and investigating with one singular goal: to ensure our local government works in partnership with the people it represents — not the institutions that seek to profit from us.

This is the Civic Mandate.
